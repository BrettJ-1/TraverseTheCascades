Cannon Beach

Geography

Cannon Beach, located along the stunning Oregon coast, is renowned for its dramatic scenery and natural beauty. The beach stretches for miles, offering expansive views of the Pacific Ocean and its ever-changing tides. At the heart of the beach is Haystack Rock, a towering sea stack that rises 235 feet above the shore, providing a striking focal point. Surrounding the beach are dense coastal forests, craggy cliffs, and tidal pools, creating a rich habitat for marine life and a perfect setting for wildlife watching. With its tranquil atmosphere and sweeping views, Cannon Beach is a popular destination for nature enthusiasts and photographers alike.

History

The region around Cannon Beach has a deep history, originally inhabited by the Clatsop and Chinook tribes who relied on the land for fishing and trade. European explorers arrived in the early 1800s, and the Lewis and Clark Expedition passed through the area in 1805. The town of Cannon Beach itself was founded in the 1800s and grew as a coastal settlement, centered around fishing and logging industries. By the mid-20th century, the town became a popular tourist destination, attracting visitors with its picturesque landscapes and its proximity to the ocean.

Culture

Cannon Beach is a small, vibrant town with a unique blend of art, culture, and natural beauty. The town is known for its art galleries, locally owned shops, and excellent seafood restaurants. It hosts several annual events such as the Cannon Beach Film Festival and the town's renowned art walks. With its welcoming atmosphere and strong artistic community, Cannon Beach offers a perfect balance of relaxation and exploration. Visitors enjoy strolling through galleries, savoring fresh local cuisine, and immersing themselves in the tranquil beauty of the coastal environment.
